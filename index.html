MORE ABOUT FUTURA 


        Futura was designed by Paul Renner in 
        1927 and was created as a contribution
        to the New Frankfurt project. The design
        is based on the simple geometries that
        became representative of the Bauhaus 
        style. Renner was not part of Bauhaus 
        but he shared their beliefs regarding 
        fonts as expressions of modernity. 
        Renner rejected the font styles of the 
        past, the grotesques, their narrowness 
        and lack of a consistent system to their
        weights and shape forms. The design of 
        Futura helped usher in a new Modern 
        age and was emblematic of the era.
